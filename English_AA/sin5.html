They do this to feel included, relevant, or accepted, letting popularity replace their own judgment. Allegory

Their punishment reflects the core of their moral failure. In life, they allowed the crowd to shape their actions, so in the afterlife the crowd is stripped away, leaving them in total isolation. They once joined in mockery when it was easy, popular, and safe, and now that same mockery surrounds them endlessly, offering no escape. Ultimately, the punishment reveals a deeper truth: blindly following harmful trends drains a person of identity, integrity, and independence until nothing remains but an echo of the voices they once imitated.
