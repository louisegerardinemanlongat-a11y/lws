Enablers

Enablers are those who, in life, allow harmful actions to continue because it benefits them, protects them, or keeps them comfortable. They remain silent while friends cheat, lie, or hurt others, cover up wrongdoing, or excuse behavior simply because it serves their own interests or strengthens social bonds. Though they may not commit the act themselves, their approval, silence, or protection makes them complicit, spreading harm indirectly. They exploit loyalty and friendship to avoid accountability, showing the selfish and opportunistic nature of humans.

Punishment

The sinners of Enabling are condemned to wander an endless, twisting maze filled with traps and punishments. They know every inch of the maze, including all hidden dangers, yet a cunning demon forces them to follow every path. No matter how much they understand or how carefully they try to avoid harm, they cannot act on their knowledge. Every turn brings new suffering, and they are compelled to endure torments they once allowed others to experience, forever trapped and powerless, knowing the way out but unable to use it.

Allegory

The maze represents the consequences of enabling others. Just as they let harm happen in life, they are now forced to follow the maze blindly, trapped by the very actions they once allowed. Their knowledge of the traps shows that they knew right from wrong but chose convenience over responsibility. The punishment teaches that supporting wrongdoing eventually traps the supporter, showing that being an enabler has real consequences.
